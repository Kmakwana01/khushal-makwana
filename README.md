## ЁЯУШ PART-A: Simple Present Tense (рк╕рк╛ркжрлЛ рк╡рк░рлНркдркорк╛рки ркХрк╛рк│)

### ЁЯз╛ 3) Sentence Structures (рк╡рк╛ркХрлНркп рк░ркЪркирк╛ркУ)
#### тЬФ (A) Affirmative (рк╣ркХрк╛рк░ рк╡рк╛ркХрлНркп)
- 5 Subjects: Subject + V1

- 4 Subjects: Subject + V1 + s/es

#### тЬФ (B) Negative (ркиркХрк╛рк░ рк╡рк╛ркХрлНркп)

#### тЬФ (C) Interrogative (рккрлНрк░рк╢рлНркирк╛рк░рлНрке)
- 5 Subjects: Do + Subject + V1?  
  Example: Do you play?
- 4 Subjects: Does + Subject + V1?

#### тЬФ (D) WH-Questions
- Structure: WH + do/does + Subject + V1?

#### тЬФ (E) "Who" Rule (Exception)
- Who = Third person singular  
- Structure: Who + V1 + s/es?

## ЁЯУШ PART-A: Uses of Most Important Modals

### 1. CAN / COULD
#### Can (Present / Future)
- Ability (рк╢ркХрлНркдрк┐): I can swim.  
- Permission (рккрк░рк╡рк╛ркиркЧрлА тАУ informal): Can I go out?  
- Possibility (рк╕рк╛ркорк╛ркирлНркп рк╢ркХрлНркпркдрк╛): It can rain today.

#### Could (Past / Polite)
- Past ability: When I was young, I could run fast.  
- Polite request: Could you help me, please?  
- Less certain possibility: He could be late.

### 2. MAY / MIGHT
#### May
- Formal permission: May I come in?  
- Possibility (50%): It may rain this evening.

#### Might
- Very low possibility (25%): It might snow in Gujarat! (very rare)  
- Past of may: He said he might come.

### 3. SHALL / SHOULD
#### Shall
- Suggestion / Offer (I/we рк╕рк╛ркерлЗ): Shall we go now?  
- Future (traditional тАУ I/we): We shall overcome.

#### Should
- Advice: You should study hard.  
- Duty / Right thing: We should respect elders.  
- Expectation: The train should arrive now.

### 4. WILL / WOULD
#### Will
- Future prediction: It will rain tomorrow.  
- Promise: I will help you.  
- Instant decision: I will answer the call.

#### Would
- Polite request: Would you mind closing the window?  
- Past habit: When I was child, I would play all day.  
- Hypothetical: If I were rich, I would travel the world.

### 5. MUST / HAVE TO
#### Must
- Strong obligation (internal): You must obey the rules.  
- Strong certainty: He must be tired (I'm sure).

#### Have to
- External obligation: I have to go to office (rule/compulsion).  
- Past: I had to finish my homework.

### 6. OUGHT TO
- Moral duty / Advice: You ought to say sorry.  
- Stronger than should, but weaker than must
